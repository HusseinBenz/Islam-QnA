Islam teaches that this life is a **test**, and that every soul will be held accountable for its deeds on the Day of Judgment.

The Islamic belief about the afterlife includes:

1. **Death** — The soul is taken by the Angel of Death. This is not the end, but a transition.
2. **The Grave (Barzakh)** — An intermediate state where the soul experiences a preview of what is to come.
3. **The Day of Judgment (Yawm al-Qiyamah)** — All of humanity will be resurrected and stand before God. Every deed, word, and intention will be accounted for.
4. **Paradise (Jannah)** — The eternal reward for those who believed and did righteous deeds. A place of unimaginable beauty, peace, and joy.
5. **Hellfire (Jahannam)** — The punishment for those who rejected God and persisted in wrongdoing.

> *"Every soul will taste death. And you will only be given your full compensation on the Day of Resurrection."* — Quran 3:185

Islam teaches that **God's mercy is vast** — He forgives all sins for those who sincerely repent.
