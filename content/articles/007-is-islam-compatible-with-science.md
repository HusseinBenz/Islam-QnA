Islam and science are not only compatible — **Islam actively encourages the pursuit of knowledge and scientific inquiry.**

The Quran contains numerous references to natural phenomena that were only confirmed by modern science centuries later:

- **Embryonic development** described in detail (Quran 23:12-14)
- **The expansion of the universe** (Quran 51:47)
- **The water cycle** (Quran 39:21)
- **Mountains as stabilizers** for the earth's crust (Quran 78:6-7)
- **The barrier between fresh and salt water** (Quran 55:19-20)

Historically, the Islamic Golden Age (8th–14th century) produced groundbreaking contributions to:

- **Algebra** (Al-Khwarizmi)
- **Optics** (Ibn al-Haytham)
- **Medicine** (Ibn Sina / Avicenna)
- **Astronomy** (Al-Biruni)
- **Chemistry** (Jabir ibn Hayyan)

The first word revealed in the Quran was **"Iqra" — Read.** Islam fundamentally values learning, inquiry, and the exploration of God's creation.
