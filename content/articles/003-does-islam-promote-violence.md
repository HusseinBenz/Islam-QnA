**Absolutely not.** Islam explicitly condemns the killing of innocent people and promotes peace, justice, and mercy.

The Quran states:

> *"Whoever kills a soul — unless for a soul or for corruption in the land — it is as if he had slain mankind entirely. And whoever saves one, it is as if he had saved mankind entirely."* — Quran 5:32

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:

> *"The Muslim is the one from whose tongue and hand the people are safe."* — Sahih al-Bukhari

Verses about fighting in the Quran were revealed in **specific historical contexts** — during war when Muslims were being persecuted, driven from their homes, and attacked. Even then, Islam set strict rules of engagement:

- Do not harm civilians, women, children, or the elderly
- Do not destroy crops or trees
- Do not force anyone to convert
- Stop fighting when the enemy seeks peace

Taking verses out of context to paint Islam as violent is intellectually dishonest and ignores the vast majority of Quranic teachings about mercy, kindness, and coexistence.
