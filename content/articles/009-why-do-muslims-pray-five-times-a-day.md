The five daily prayers (Salah) are the **most important act of worship** in Islam after the declaration of faith. They serve as a direct connection between the servant and God — no intermediary needed.

The five prayers are:

- **Fajr** — Before sunrise
- **Dhuhr** — Midday
- **Asr** — Afternoon
- **Maghrib** — After sunset
- **Isha** — Night

Why five times?

- They create a **rhythm of remembrance** throughout the day
- They prevent a person from becoming absorbed in worldly distractions
- They provide moments of **peace, reflection, and gratitude**
- They remind the Muslim of their purpose and accountability

The Prophet ﷺ compared the five prayers to a river flowing by your door — if you bathed in it five times a day, no dirt would remain on you. Similarly, the five prayers **purify the soul** from sin and heedlessness.

> *"Indeed, prayer prohibits immorality and wrongdoing."* — Quran 29:45
